Uniswap
How it works

Uniswap is a decentralized exchange where all liquidity on the platform is provided by liquidity providers (LPs). They supply their liquidity in any ERC20 token and in return, LPs get some of the fees that Uniswap charges. The catch is that every LP needs to supply the assets for both sides of the pool.
Basically what Uniswap allows people to do is anyone in the world can be a market maker by sending their assets to a smart contract, but you have to abide by a market-making equation where the price of an asset in the pool is X * Y = K where X and Y are the tokens in the pair and K is a constant that never changes. It’s also important to note that Uniswap markets have no idea what the price of a pair is on another exchange. The price of an asset on Uniswap is solely determined by the X * Y = K formula.

About Us

In 2019, it was estimated that decentralized exchange (DEX) arbitrage was a $10M+ million industry, but in 2020 we’re now seeing that volumes are up ~30x since. Large firms, such as Alameda Research, are creating 100s of millions of private value by using proprietary arbitrage and market making strategies to profit on variance between the bid/ask spread or the mispricing between DEX’s and/or centralized exchanges.
Uniswap, and other projects such as Balancer, have made it possible for anyone in the world to become a market maker using an automated market maker (AMM) strategy on a decentralized exchange. The problem is that these strategies happen in a vacuum, and without cross-dex liquidity and arbitrage, the DEX market would be severely mispriced. Uniswap 2.0 uses a capital inefficient algorithm that requires traders to ensure their pricing matches other markets. In their documentation, Uniswap lists traders and arbitrage bots as key industry participants that “help equalize prices across broader Ethereum markets and keep things fair.”
In the world of market making, high frequency trading, and arbitrage, profitable strategies have often been held close to the chest by large institutions with significant infrastructure.
Given this scenario the UAT platform was born, with the goal to provide alpha for investors in our “arbitrage pools” (AP) focusing on cross-DEX and DEX:CEX arbitrage opportunities to provide.
